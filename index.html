About me
Hi, I’m Helen Marshall – fashion designer, mother of three, grandmother of six, and a lifelong lover of beautiful fabrics, bold prints, and well-brewed tea.

I was born in Liverpool in 1950, the oldest of five lively siblings. From a young age, I showed a dangerous combination of curiosity and scissors – my poor mother would find her dresses mysteriously shortened or “improved.” What can I say? Fashion called early.

 

I moved to London in my twenties and built my dream from the ground up – co-founding a boutique fashion studio specializing in bridal gowns and custom eveningwear.

My designs were always about celebrating real women: curves, quirks, confidence and all. Over the years, I had the privilege of dressing brides, performers, businesswomen, and even the occasional diva – and I loved every second of it.

 

Beyond the sketchbook and sewing machine, I’ve always been a people person. I believe a great outfit can change how you walk into a room – and I’ve seen it happen time and time again. Today, in my fabulous 70s, I continue to inspire and be inspired: I host styling workshops for women over 60, volunteer with organizations that support older women, and I still design the occasional custom piece (old habits die hard).

 

My philosophy? Style has no expiration date. If it makes you feel radiant – wear it. And if there’s music playing – dance.

Q&A
List three main differences between Web1, Web2, and Web3 – use the material covered in lectures and in the internet.
1. Type of Interaction:
- Web1: Read-only – users can only view content.
- Web2: Read and write – users can create, share, and interact.
- Web3: Read, write, and own – users control and own their data and content.

2. Data Ownership:
- Web1: Controlled by website owners.
- Web2: Controlled by centralized platforms (e.g., Facebook, Google).
- Web3: Controlled by users through decentralized technologies (e.g., blockchain).

3. Technology and Experience:
- Web1: Static websites with basic structure.
- Web2: Dynamic, interactive interfaces and user-generated content.
- Web3: Intelligent, personalized, and decentralized experiences using AI and smart contracts.
Explain the differences mentioned in question 1 using an example of a use case – for example: a search query for "symptoms of the flu" would look one way in Web1 and a different way in Web3.
Use Case: Searching for "recommended cafes in Tel Aviv"

1. Web1:
- You visit a static webpage with a basic list of cafes in Tel Aviv.
- The page contains outdated or generic information.
- No photos, reviews, or user interaction.

2. Web2:
- You search on Google and view results from Google Maps or TripAdvisor.
- You can see user reviews, photos, ratings, and even ask questions.
- You can leave your own review and share with friends.

3. Web3:
- You use a decentralized app (DApp) that gives personalized cafe recommendations.
- The app accesses your preferences (e.g., vegan-friendly, quiet, open now) securely through your digital wallet.
- You get token rewards for contributing reviews, and your data stays private and owned by you.
